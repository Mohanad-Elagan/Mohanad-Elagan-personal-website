Community Detector in Tnetwork

The difficulty with the overlapping community detection approach is effectively mining both topological and temporal structures. Community and change point detection are the most effective approaches for studying such developing structural relationships. Therefore, we use an algorithm that uses the so-called change points within communities to find overlapping community structures. The suggested technique combines two methods that can effectively identify stable communities at various time scales, in contrast to existing dynamic community identification approaches. The first method involves adding a community to the given dynamic graphs to detect communities on a multi-temporal scale. In order to track the expanded community and improve the algorithm, the second method involves computing the NMIs and plotting the found communities in conjugate with the first method. The efficacy of the technique is tested on high-resolution time-varying and artificial contact networks made from existing social networks.

DOey

A Business Project.

The application is not completed yet. However, 70% is completed, and the rest is under development to be published when it is done. It will allow adolescents to learn about real-life professions and work on them online. The application will provide a wide range of jobs, including writing, web development, graphic design, and many more. Each applicant will be able to choose from any of the available jobs. Following the job selection, and acceptance exam will be provided to assess the applicant's level and whether or not the applicant reached the required level for the selected job. Consequently, if the required level is met, the applicant will be allowed to begin work; if not, applicants will be offered courses to learn more about the chosen job, and they will have the opportunity to retake an acceptance exam. Furthermore, both applicant levels will have the opportunity to take the courses if they see that they will not perform well in the acceptance exam. I've also established a rating system for the doers, which the customer will assign based on the doer's quality. Therefore, this system will specialize each doer based on the rating stars. I aim to use gamification techniques to offer applicants motivation. To be more specific, a leaderboard will be provided in the application based on the applicants' ratings over the course of a week or month, with awards awarded to the top three. This idea will help maintain a competitive community of doers in the application and motivate many more young people to work, even if it is only for enjoyment, like in a game. I have provided a communication system for the application. This communication system will help coordinate work between the customer and the worker.

Revilky

The COVID-19 pandemics affected the Economy of various countries causing many troubles in their economic system. A comparative analysis was done between some continents taking their major countries, for example, the USA, France, and Japan before and after COVID-19 in two different criteria, economic impacts and air quality. For instance, more than one billion passengers in the USA were removed representing a decline of 64.4% of global passenger traffic due to COVID-19. In France, the amount of NO2, NO, and CO levels in the air, were reduced by 67%, 78%, and 62%, respectively, resulting in an 80% reduction in road traffic.

Data & Resources: EO dashboard, OSM, OSM overpass API.

1.     Using the tax system to provide incentives for better social outcomes, such as tobacco and carbon taxes, and smart earmarking of taxes to support social programs in education and health.

2.     Increasing the tax burden on affluent households by taxing real estate and capital gains.

3.     Establishing minimum tax payment levels and progressive personal income tax regimes that help reduce income disparity.

Furthermore, the critical economic policy is exchange rate flexibility. A more flexible currency rate might improve competitiveness, stimulate international investment and tourists, and assist growing industries in exporting. These have the ability to provide jobs and long-term growth, hence improving living standards. In addition, the fluctuation of the Egyptian pound's exchange rate against the US dollar is a positive sign.

Reforms to the structure are also critical. It will be critical to make structural changes to enhance the business climate and make Egypt more appealing to investors. Building contemporary and open institutions to foster accountability and good governance and ensure fair and transparent game rules is significant for the government. A fair economic policy may ensure that the benefits of growth reach all members of society. It can ensure that everyone has equal access to jobs and business possibilities, regardless of whether or not they are connected. There are also initiatives authorities may take to improve the financial system's performance. Only 10% of Egyptians, for example, have bank accounts. Increasing the availability of financial services and providing more financing to small firms that create jobs might significantly positively impact the economy.

Finally, improving the educational system for Egypt's long-term development is essential. Jobs cannot be produced until the educational system is aligned to equip graduates for the skills required in today's work market. If properly implemented, the constitutionally mandated rebalance of expenditure toward health and education might support higher, more equitable growth in the long run while maintaining financial sustainability.

Overall, the current status of Egypt and other countries is getting better through the excellent distribution of income and wealth between its people. Regardless that the situation in Egypt is getting better, as president El Sisi is working and focusing on solving the problem of inequality in Egypt, there are still other countries that counter this problem, and they do not receive help. However, with the right policies and equality, Egypt and other countries' governments can meet the hopes and aspirations of its people.

School vs. Real-world

At the beginning of each student's life, students are enrolled in many different classes. They are expected to acquire and memorize principles in science, mathematics, physics, and other subjects. They move from one lesson to another without understanding why they are required to learn these lessons or even these subjects. They know that if they want to go to the best colleges, they must achieve the highest marks. They get addicted to education by spending their time completing homework and tasks without even understanding it. They do not have any doubts about their educational system. Some of the students may have had the talent to be an actor or a passion for painting, but these students are never encouraged to pursue their passions. In response to the common flawed educational system between the countries, the educational system must be based on life application skills that will help students know what is beyond their subjects in the 'Real-world.'

I.                Preparation for college and career life: schools must help students identify their future careers.

Although education is critical for many careers, it is unnecessary for everyone or every career, as students demand life skills such as personal responsibility and the ability to handle their finances. Therefore, schools must provide courses that will prepare students for any path they choose in life, including both college and career preparation.

In particular, this can be done by requesting the alumni of every school to give online night sessions for the students to discuss and share their life experiences with them to avoid their failures after finishing or during their school. Moreover, they can also tell them more about college and how to prepare for college applications.

II.             Encourage teamwork: schools must teach their students how to work in teams and be effective leaders in the community.

Working as part of a team is one of the most important skills that students nowadays need to thrive in the workplace. They must be able to negotiate, compromise, and share credit in order to contribute to projects as a productive contributor. These skills may be taught in school by requiring students to work in groups on certain projects and assignments.
Furthermore, the idea of teamwork can be conducted in the classes, which will make it easier for students to understand the subjects and share the ideas by the technique of 'Think-pair-share.'

However, finding a career that does not need you to be able to work closely and happily with others can also be challenging. Traditional collaboration is necessary to prepare students in this area adequately. Rather than forming a group and assigning responsibilities to one another, students should be encouraged to take on diverse roles within their group for each project. Depending on the situation, they can be an 'writer' or a 'leader.' Students can occupy various roles with their classmates during a project, allowing them to engage with others in a more collaborative manner.

 

III.           Provide students with real-life skills: skills taught in school are different from the skills in the real world outside the school.

Arguably, students who have recently graduated from high school lack skills. They do not know how to deal with the real world. If they obtained any skills throughout their high school years, they did from part-time jobs to earn money. Otherwise, the school does not contribute to the students' learning of necessary skills. Students' core problem after graduation is a lack of understanding of how money works. There isn't a class in school that teaches you how to save, manage, or invest money. Even after excelling in their mathematics and finance classes, many students struggle to manage their funds. This issue is due to the fact that school courses are entirely theoretical, and nothing in those textbooks teaches students to be practical!
Therefore, providing the students with many extracurricular activities and internship programs will help them gain real-world skills.

Most schools in Slovakia fail to prepare their students for working life properly. 'I don't think my schools helped me much to prepare for a working life,' said Silvia Kov'čov', when she did not find job opportunities after long searching. According to the 'Quarter to Quality Education' study conducted by the Slovak Accreditation Agency for Higher Education (SAAHE) between late April and late May 2021, 41% of public university students believe their schools did not do enough to help their graduates find work. On the other hand, private university students feel more equipped for life and work in the future, with just 15% stating they are poorly prepared. Moreover, college dropout rates steadily increase over time because of the flawed educational system between countries. As the report highlights in the United States, the overall dropout rate for undergraduate college students is 40%, and in 4-year colleges, 56% of students drop out within six years.

Meanwhile, the epidemic has made it more difficult for graduates with no work experience to obtain work. Apart from the lack of a traineeship, the respondents express dissatisfaction with the lack of opportunities to strengthen their foreign language and transferrable skills while still in school.

Indeed, our schools nowadays do not prepare students for the real world. However, several countries have begun to work on improvements; for example, "Leaf Academy" is built to strengthen real-world skills (e.g., leadership and self-discipline) and prepare foreign and Slovakian students for college or career life. Thus, there is a hope that the educational system will be improved in the near future.
